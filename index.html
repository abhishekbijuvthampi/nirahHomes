Overview

Welcome to Nirah Homes, where the hills of Kuttikkanam wrap you in a gentle hush and the mornings greet you with a blanket of mist. Tucked away in this quiet corner of Kerala, Nirah isn’t just a place to stay — it’s a feeling. The air is crisp, often cooler than 16°C, and the scent of damp earth and fresh leaves lingers as you step out onto the porch with a warm cup of tea in hand. Time seems to slow here. Birds call through the fog, distant hills peek through the clouds, and everything feels a little softer, a little quieter. Ammachi Kottaram
Built in the 19th century by Maharaja Moolam Thirunal, Ammachi Kottaram was a royal retreat set in misty highlands, with grand verandas and hidden tunnels.
Parunthumpara
Parunthumpara is a serene hill viewpoint in Idukki, Kerala, known for its misty views, fresh air, and as a Sabarimala Makarajyothi viewing spot.
CSI Church
Built in 1869, St. George CSI Church in Pallikkunnu is one of Idukki’s oldest, established on land gifted by Maharani Sethulakshmibai and completed by Henry Baker Jr.
Pine forest Thattathikkanam
Located near Kuttikkanam in Idukki, the Thattathikkanam Pine Forest is now a beautiful eco-tourism spot. Spread over nine hectares, this serene pine valley is right along the Kottarakara-Dindigul Highway.
Valanjanganam Waterfalls
Valanjanganam Falls, near Kuttikkanam in Idukki, is a refreshing roadside waterfall just 1 km from Murinjapuzha, a perfect spot for a quick break, hot tea, and scenic views on your journey.
Urumbikkara
Urumbikkara, near Kuttikkanam in Idukki, is a scenic hill station known for its misty views, off-road jeep trails, and tea-covered hills , perfect for adventure, nature walks, and cool mountain air.
Peelikunnu
Peelikunnu is a peaceful hill spot near Peermade in Idukki, Kerala, offering calm surroundings and beautiful views. This hidden gem features a rocky peak perfect for soaking in the beauty of nature.
