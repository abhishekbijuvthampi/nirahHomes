Where Nature Meets Home

Overview

Welcome to Nirah Homes, where the hills of Kuttikkanam wrap you in a gentle hush and the mornings greet you with a blanket of mist. Tucked away in this quiet corner of Kerala, Nirah isn’t just a place to stay — it’s a feeling. The air is crisp, often cooler than 16°C, and the scent of damp earth and fresh leaves lingers as you step out onto the porch with a warm cup of tea in hand. Time seems to slow here. Birds call through the fog, distant hills peek through the clouds, and everything feels a little softer, a little quieter. Whether you’re curling up with a book, sharing stories by the fire, or wandering down leafy trails, Nirah Homes offers a simple kind of peace — one that stays with you long after you’ve left.

Ammachi Kottaram
Built in the 19th century by Travancore Maharaja Moolam Thirunal, Ammachi Kottaram was a royal retreat for the Maharaja’s wife. Nestled in the misty tea and coffee highlands, the palace sits on 25 scenic acres with beautiful verandas, spacious halls, and even secret tunnels!
Parunthumpara
Parunthumpara is a beautiful hill viewpoint in Idukki, Kerala, near Peermade and Thekkady. Visitors come here for stunning views, fresh mountain air, and spots like Suicide Point and Tagor Head rock. It’s a peaceful stop on the way to Thekkady or Munnar. During the Sabarimala season, many pilgrims gather to see the Makarajyothi light from here.
CSI Church
Built by the British in 1869, St. George CSI Church in Pallikkunnu, Idukki, is one of the oldest churches under the Diocese of East Kerala. The land was donated by Travancore Maharani Rani Sethulakshmibai, and the church was completed by Henry Baker Jr. and other plantation owners.
Pine forest Thattathikkanam
Located near Kuttikkanam in Idukki, the Thattathikkanam Pine Forest is now a beautiful eco-tourism spot. Spread over nine hectares, this serene pine valley is right along the Kottarakara-Dindigul Highway.
Valanjanganam Waterfalls
Valanjanganam Falls, also called Valanjanganam Vellachaattam, is a beautiful roadside waterfall near Kuttikkanam in Idukki. Just 1 km from Murinjapuzha on the Kottayam-Kumily highway, it’s a perfect spot to take a break, sip some tea, and enjoy the refreshing view — an ideal quick stop to relax during your road trip!
Urumbikkara
Urumbikkara near Kuttikkanam is a beautiful hill station in Idukki, known for its misty hills, scenic viewpoints, and exciting off-road jeep trails. Visitors can enjoy bumpy rides through tea plantations and forests, with breathtaking views all around. It’s a great spot for adventure, nature walks, and relaxing in cool mountain air.
Peelikunnu
Peelikunnu is a stunning Hill area which can help you spend few hours at peace. With it's great calm and beautiful view this place can definitely bring a smile on your face. It is a hidden gem located in Peermade near Kuttikanam in Idukki district of Kerala. The peak of the hill is a rocky area, from where nature can be enjoyed at its best.
